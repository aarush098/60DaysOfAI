## SECTION 12: Interview Talking Points

### Most Impressive Achievements (lead with these)
1. **30% efficiency improvement** on the E-Book Reading Platform through user behavior data analysis — your strongest, most quantified story
2. **25% reduction in navigation time** — pairs well with #1 as a UI/UX-informed-by-data narrative
3. **100+ cities of real-time data processed and validated** on the Weather App — shows you can handle data at some scale, plus data validation logic (a real analyst skill)
4. **Campus Ambassador reaching 100+ students** — good stakeholder communication/scale story if asked about "working with others" or "influence without authority"

### Key Stories to Prepare (STAR format)
- **The E-Book Platform story:** What data did you look at? What specific UX decision changed? How did you measure the 30%? (Be ready — interviewers will ask "how did you measure this," so know your actual method in detail)
- **The Weather App validation story:** What kinds of data errors were you catching? How did you decide on the validation logic? This shows data quality thinking, which recruiters value highly.
- **A "self-directed learning" story:** Be ready to explain *why* you're learning Power BI/Tableau/Python now and *how* you're structuring that learning — this reframes "in progress" skills as initiative, not a weakness.

### Areas Recruiters Are Likely to Probe
- **"You don't have Power BI/Tableau/Excel project experience yet — how would you ramp up?"** → Have your learning roadmap (Section 10) ready as a real answer, not a vague promise.
- **"Your projects are web apps, not data analysis projects — walk me through your analytical process on one of them."** → Be very specific about what data you looked at and how, since this is the gap they'll press on most.
- **"Do you have experience with large/structured/business datasets?"** → Be honest: no, but describe your plan (the roadmap) and any relevant coursework/statistics fundamentals.
- **"What's your SQL level?"** → Be ready to describe the actual queries/joins/aggregations you've written, not just "SQL" as a checkbox.
- **GPA (6.58/10):** Some interviewers may ask about this — have a short, confident, non-defensive answer ready (e.g., balancing coursework with hands-on project building).
