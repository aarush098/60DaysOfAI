Here are some of the most effective, evidence-backed ways to boost productivity:

**Manage your energy, not just your time**
Work with your natural rhythms — tackle demanding tasks during your peak focus hours and save routine work for low-energy periods.

**Time-block your calendar**
Schedule specific blocks for deep work, admin, and meetings. Unstructured time tends to get consumed by reactive tasks.

**Use the two-minute rule**
If something takes less than two minutes, do it immediately rather than adding it to a list.

**Limit work in progress**
Multitasking is a myth — switching costs are real. Focus on finishing one thing before starting the next.

**Take intentional breaks**
The Pomodoro Technique (25 min work / 5 min break) works because rest is part of performance, not a reward for it.

**Reduce decision fatigue**
Standardize routines, meals, and recurring choices so your mental energy goes toward work that actually matters.

**Protect your mornings**
Many high performers delay email and meetings until mid-morning, using the first hours for their most important work.

**Write things down immediately**
Your brain is for thinking, not storage. Capture tasks, ideas, and commitments in a trusted system so you're not mentally juggling them.

**Do a weekly review**
Spend 15–30 minutes each week clearing your inbox, reviewing your goals, and planning the coming week. It prevents drift.

**Say no more often**
Every commitment is a trade-off. Protecting your time from low-value requests is one of the highest-leverage things you can do.
The biggest gains usually come not from working more hours, but from being more intentional about *how* you spend them.
