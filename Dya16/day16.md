Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) — India's largest IT services company, part of the Tata Group, with strong exposure to BFSI, consumer, and manufacturing verticals globally.

CMP: ₹1,983 | Market Cap: ₹7,16,423 Cr | P/E: 13.7x (Screener)

Valuation verdict: Cheap — TCS has historically traded near 25–30x earnings; the current 13.7x is well below both its own 5-year average and IT-sector peers, reflecting the stock's sharp correction (down ~42% over 1 year, per Screener's price data).

D/E: Very low, borrowings are a small fraction of reserves — comfortably safe.

ROE: 51.8% | ROCE: 63.0% — exceptional, among the best in Indian large-caps.

Growth trend: Steady but slowing — 5Y sales CAGR ~10%, but TTM sales growth just ~5%, and profit growth has been modest (8% 3Y CAGR).

Strengths: Best-in-class capital efficiency (ROE/ROCE); consistently high dividend payout (~77–94%) with a 3.23% yield; strong free cash flow generation (~₹48,000 Cr in FY26).

Watch-points: Revenue growth has clearly decelerated versus its historical pace, flagged by Screener as "poor 5Y sales growth"; the stock's steep price decline suggests the market is pricing in continued demand softness in global IT spending.

Fundamental Quality: Strong — TCS remains financially rock-solid with elite return ratios and low debt, but growth momentum has cooled, which is likely behind the valuation de-rating.

🚩 Promoter holding 71.77%, no pledging reported (Screener).
