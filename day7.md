Here's your personalized Claude strategy! A few things worth highlighting beyond the visual:

Why Sonnet is your best friend as a student coder

it's genuinely excellent at explaining why code works, not just giving you the answer. 
That distinction matters a lot when you're learning, since understanding the reasoning is what sticks for exams and interviews.

A daily workflow that works well:

Start your study/coding session with Sonnet at Standard

If you hit a wall after 15 minutes on a problem → bump to High effort

If it's still unsolved and genuinely complex (not just unfamiliar) → switch to Opus

Use Haiku for anything where you just need a quick fact

One bonus tip:

when asking for debugging help, share: the error message, what you expected to happen, and what actually happened. 
That context makes Claude's answers 10x more useful than just pasting code.
